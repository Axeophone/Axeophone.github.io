Personal Brand Style Guide

Ryan Alford — Cloud Infrastructure & UX Design Professional

"Reliable by nature, future-focused by trade, always leveling up."

Brand Overview

The foundation and purpose behind the Ryan Alford brand.

Mission

To build reliable, user-centered technology systems and design experiences that make complexity feel effortless.

Vision

To be a trusted technology professional at the intersection of cloud infrastructure and human-centered design.

Values

Reliability, continuous learning, precision, collaboration, and genuine curiosity about how systems work.

Logo

The RA monogram and wordmark. Use consistently across all brand materials.

White / Light Background

White Glove Background

Near Black Background

Professional Blue Background

Do

  • Use the RA monogram consistently as the primary mark
  • Maintain clear space equal to the height of the "R" on all sides
  • Use Professional Blue (#185FA5) as the accent color on the "A"
  • Use on approved backgrounds (white, off-white, near black)

Don't

  • Don't stretch, skew, or distort the logo
  • Don't change the logo colors arbitrarily
  • Don't place on busy backgrounds without sufficient contrast
  • Don't use outdated versions of the mark

Do

  • Use Professional Blue as the primary action color (buttons, links)
  • Use Near Black for all primary text and headings
  • Use Sensible Red sparingly for calls-to-action or critical highlights
  • Pair Blue Tint with Near Black text for section backgrounds

Don't

  • Don't use Sensible Red as a background color for large areas
  • Don't use Professional Blue for body text — it reduces readability
  • Don't introduce colors outside this palette without justification
  • Don't place Near Black text on Professional Blue backgrounds

Voice & Tone

How Ryan Alford communicates across platforms and contexts.

Reliable

Confident, clear, and consistent. Delivers on what is promised.

Curious

Genuinely interested in how systems work and how they can be improved.

Accessible

Clear communicator. Avoids jargon when possible. Explains the "why."

Growth-Oriented

Always learning, always improving. Comfortable acknowledging limits and expanding them.

Write Like This

  • "I build systems that teams can count on."
  • "Here's what I learned from that failure..."
  • "Let me walk you through the process I used."
  • "I'm still learning X, but here's what I know so far."

Avoid This

  • Buzzword-heavy phrases with no substance
  • Passive voice that avoids accountability
  • Overclaiming expertise in areas still developing
  • Cold, corporate language that feels impersonal
